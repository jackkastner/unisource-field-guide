A new tool. An honest walkthrough.

What if every account manager and designer had a junior designer and front-end developer on call — and they worked at the speed of typing?

That's roughly what this is. You describe what you want. It builds a working version in HTML — clickable, shareable, exportable to PowerPoint or PDF. You iterate until it's right. This page is itself an example of the output.

03

The workflow, in three moves.

No onboarding. If you can write a Slack message, you can brief it.

01

Describe it

Plain English. "A one-pager pitching our services to a 200-person law firm moving offices. Include our case studies, the phased process, and pricing ranges."

02

Attach context

Drop in brand assets, past decks, client logos, product imagery, a screenshot of a reference you like — or a link to a codebase. More context, better output.

03

Iterate & ship

Comment on any element. Ask for variants. Export to PowerPoint, PDF, or a shareable link. Revise as the client feedback rolls in.
